Full Bio Of
Shane Scarbrough

I love to problem solve.
At an early age I noticed how much I enjoyed math and the complexity of advanced math. As I began to learn new methods of math, such as multiplication and division, math became my favorite subject and I wanted to learn efficiant ways to handle math problems. During high school I became interested in learning more about computers and how they worked. I tried a maintenance class that taught me how to build my first computer and then quickly found out I cared more about what the computer could do once it was on and less about how to build it. Always facinated by coding, I thought it would be too difficult to learn and never decided to pursue a career in it.

Needing a job to start a career I thought that I could work in a refinary like the rest of my family. After 2 years of working in that field I relized I wanted something different, something that was new and a little challenging. During covid, I wanted to learn something new so I became a leasing agent and noticed that I really liked to talk to customers and try to advacate for them. After a year of leasing, the management saw potential in me and promoted me to assistant property manager. In this new role I found more challenges that I welcomed. I learned to communicate with clients and and contractors more efficiantlly, I became more efficiant with computers learning to type faster and troubleshoot a whole new system.

I continued at for 2 years and noticed I was no longer growning in my skills and the challenges were fading. During the 2 years I had gotten married and wanted to try and find a career to support my new wife. Having a background in Ultrasonic testing, I wanted to try and make that my full career. I knew the money would be good and I was sure to find new challenges. New challenges was an understatement, I was able to pick up the information needed for this new job pretty quick. However the main challenge was that I would be traveling around the world to job sites. Newly married I wasn't that excited about this, but I made the most of it and tried to advance my career into more advanced testing.

A little less than a year later, I found it extreamly hard to leave my wife for weeks at a time constantly. After long consideration, we decided it would be best for me to leave ultrasonic testing behind me. With little to no idea where to go from there, I had a friend who tried to get me into coding years ago and I struggled to think I could do it. He encourged me to try Codecademy, saying it was a good website to get a foundation started. One month into learning code, I began to realize that this could be something I do for a career. Endless challenges, building projects from scratch, and language after language, it felt like there would be a new challenge everyday.

Learning so much in a short time, I began building my first project followed by a portfolio to show off the project. I wanted to start with something familiar and a calculator was a project that many people recommended for early programmers. It gave me much needed confidence after finishing my first project, something I once thought would be too difficult now something I would be lucky to do for the rest of my life.
